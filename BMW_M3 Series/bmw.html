BMW M3 Series
BMW E36 M3 is a high-performance version of the BMW 3 Series, produced from 1992 to 1999. It was available in coupe, sedan, and convertible body styles. The E36 M3 is powered by a 3.0L or 3.2L inline-six engine, depending on the model year and market. It features a sport-tuned suspension, larger brakes, and distinctive M3 styling elements. The E36 M3 is known for its balanced handling and powerful engine, making it a popular choice among driving enthusiasts. It has a rich motorsport history, with successful racing campaigns in various series around the world. The BMW E46 is a compact executive car manufactured by BMW from 1998 to 2006. It is the fourth generation of the 3 Series and succeeded the E36. The E46 was a significant departure from its predecessor in terms of design, performance, and technology. It featured a more aerodynamic body with a longer wheelbase and wider track. The interior was more spacious and luxurious, and it came with a variety of new features, including a navigation system, a sunroof, and heated seats. The E46 was powered by a variety of engines, including four-cylinder, six-cylinder, and eight-cylinder options. The most powerful engine was the 3.2-liter inline-six, which produced 333 horsepower.

The BMW E92 M3 is a high-performance variant of the BMW 3 Series that was produced from 2007 to 2013. It is the fourth generation of the M3, and was available in coupe, sedan, and convertible body styles. The E92 M3 is powered by a 4.0-liter V8 engine that produces 414 horsepower and 295 pound-feet of torque. It is paired with a six-speed manual transmission or a seven-speed dual-clutch transmission. The E92 M3 can accelerate from 0 to 60 mph in 4.6 seconds and has a top track speed of 155 mph. The E92 M3 is a well-rounded sports car that offers excellent performance, handling, and comfort.

The M3 version of the F30 3 Series was designated the F80 and was produced from 2014 to 2018.[141] The F80 M3 was powered by the BMW S55 twin-turbocharged straight-six engine, therefore being the first turbocharged M3 model. Despite the smaller displacement than the V8 engine used by the previous generation of M3, the switch from naturally aspirated engines to turbocharging resulted in peak power being increased from 309 to 317 kW (420 to 431 PS; 414 to 425 hp), and peak torque being increased from 400 to 550 N⋅m (295 to 406 lb⋅ft). The official 0–97 km/h (0–60 mph) acceleration times are 3.9 seconds with the M-DCT transmission and 4.1 seconds with the manual transmission.

At launch in 2020, the M3 Competition model was unveiled alongside the standard M3 model. Compared to the standard M3, the M3 Competition increases power output by 22 kW (30 PS; 30 hp) to a total of 375 kW (510 PS; 503 hp) and torque is increased by 100 N⋅m (74 lb⋅ft) to 650 N⋅m (479 lb⋅ft), and it is offered exclusively with an 8-speed M Steptronic Sport automatic transmission. The M3 Competition also features a separate transmission oil cooler, black chrome exhaust tips, forged M light-alloy wheels, automatic brake hold function, and high-gloss black mirrors.
